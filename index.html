Resource: Resilience, Opportunity,Safety, Education, Strength (ROSES) Program

Posted | Jul 21 2022

This community based, trauma informed, gender- responsive advocacy interventions* is designed for girls 11 to 17 years old who are at risk for or already involved in the juvenile justice system. The program is rated Promising in the Crime Solutions database. Intervention group girls were less likely to engage in physical fights and minor status offending behavior (i.e., missing fewer days of school), compared with control group girls. These differences were statistically significant. Learn more.

Share with Youth: Next Legends

This website* is a home to a new youth e-cigarette prevention campaign that aims to educate American Indian and Alaska Native (AI/AN) youth, ages 12- 17, about the harms of vaping. Mission Statement

Children of Incarcerated Parents is a website that helps you establish, maintain, and develope effective youth programs. The site includes youth facts, funding information, and tools to help you assess community resources, produce maps of local and federal resources, search for evidence-based youth programs, and keep up-to-date on the latest, youth-related topics.
